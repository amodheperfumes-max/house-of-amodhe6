Café Ambré

Café Ambré is an extrait de parfum composed as a slow, deliberate study of warmth. It is built around roasted coffee beans that feel dark, dry, and gently bitter rather than sweet. The opening is intentionally restrained, avoiding brightness or sharp contrasts.

As the fragrance settles, warm amber begins to soften the coffee, adding depth and a quiet resinous glow. The composition stays close to the skin, evolving gradually instead of projecting loudly. There is no rush, no dramatic shift, only a steady unfolding.

This fragrance is designed for time rather than attention. Café Ambré does not announce itself. It lingers softly, becoming part of the wearer rather than the room.

Craft and Time

At House of Amodhe, time is treated as a material rather than a constraint. Each composition is allowed to rest, evolve, and mature before it is considered complete.

We do not accelerate formulas to meet launch schedules or seasonal demands. Maturation is a fundamental part of our process, allowing rough edges to soften and the fragrance to settle into balance.

This approach results in compositions that feel calm, integrated, and natural on the skin. Nothing is rushed. Nothing is forced.

Edition Statement

Café Ambré is released once every month in a strictly limited edition of twenty bottles. When the allocation is complete, the fragrance is not reproduced until the next cycle.

This structure is intentional. Limitation allows attention to remain on quality, consistency, and involvement at every stage of production.

Scarcity is not used as a marketing device, but as a practical framework that protects care and process.

Quiet Questions

Is Café Ambré gendered?
No. Café Ambré is composed without gender in mind. It responds to skin chemistry rather than identity.

How long does it last?
As an extrait de parfum, it develops slowly and remains present on the skin for many hours without becoming overwhelming.

Is it available year round?
No. Café Ambré is released once per month in limited quantities. Once a batch is sold out, the next release must be awaited.
